Privacy Policy

Effective date: 6 May 2026  ·  Last updated: 6 May 2026

Advora ("Advora", "we", "us", or "our") provides a software platform that helps businesses manage their social media presence on Meta platforms (Facebook and Instagram), including analytics, content scheduling, and ad insights. This Privacy Policy explains what information we collect, how we use and store it, who we share it with, and the choices and rights you have. It applies to all users of the Advora web application available at https://ekhadijewels-demo.advora.in and any related services (collectively, the "Service").

1. Who we are

Advora is the data controller for personal information collected directly from registered users of the Service. When you connect a third-party account (such as a Facebook Page, Instagram Business account, or Meta Ad Account) to Advora, we act as a data processor for the data we receive from those platforms on your behalf.

2. Information we collect

2.1 Information you provide

  • Account information: name, email address, password (hashed), workspace/organization name.
  • Billing information processed by our payment provider (we do not store full card numbers).
  • Support communications you send to us.

2.2 Information we receive from Meta when you connect an account

When you click "Connect Meta" inside Advora and authorize access through Facebook Login, we receive only the data covered by the permissions you grant. Depending on the scopes you approve, this may include:

  • Your Facebook user ID, name, and email (basic profile).
  • List of Facebook Pages you administer (pages_show_list).
  • Page access tokens and Page metadata (pages_read_engagement).
  • Connected Instagram Business account ID, username, profile picture, follower counts, and media (instagram_basic).
  • Insights and analytics for your Pages, Instagram accounts, and posts (instagram_manage_insights, read_insights).
  • Business Manager assets you control (business_management).
  • Ad account read-only data such as campaign performance, where authorized (ads_read).

We do not collect or store your Facebook password. We do not access private messages, your friends' personal data, or content from accounts you do not administer.

2.3 Information collected automatically

  • Log data: IP address, browser type, pages visited, timestamps.
  • Cookies and similar technologies used to keep you signed in and to remember preferences.
  • Aggregated usage analytics (no individual identification).

3. How we use your information

  • To provide, operate, and improve the Service.
  • To display analytics, scheduled posts, and insights for the Meta accounts you have connected.
  • To authenticate you and secure your account.
  • To send transactional messages (e.g. password resets, billing receipts, service notifications).
  • To comply with legal obligations and enforce our Terms of Service.

We do not sell your personal information. We do not use data obtained via Meta APIs for advertising, profiling, or to train AI models without your explicit consent.

4. How we store and protect your data

  • Data is stored on secured servers operated by our hosting provider.
  • Access tokens received from Meta are stored encrypted at rest and transmitted over TLS.
  • Access to production data is restricted to authorized personnel and logged.
  • We retain your data for as long as your account is active. On account deletion, we delete or anonymize your personal data within 30 days, except where retention is required by law.

5. Sharing your information

We share information only in the following limited circumstances:

  • With service providers acting on our behalf (hosting, email delivery, payment processing) under contractual confidentiality obligations.
  • With Meta, when you initiate actions that call Meta APIs (e.g. publishing a post you scheduled).
  • If required by law, regulation, legal process, or governmental request.
  • In connection with a merger, acquisition, or sale of assets — with notice to affected users.

6. Your rights and choices

  • Disconnect a Meta account at any time inside Advora at Settings → Connections → Disconnect. This revokes our stored access tokens.
  • Revoke access from Meta directly at facebook.com/settings?tab=business_tools.
  • Access, correct, export, or delete your personal data — email us at the address below.
  • Withdraw consent for non-essential processing.
  • Residents of the EU/UK have rights under the GDPR; California residents have rights under the CCPA. We honour applicable rights regardless of where you live.

7. Data deletion instructions (for Meta)

How to request deletion of your data:

  1. Email privacy@advora.in from the address associated with your Advora account, with subject line "Data deletion request".
  2. We will delete all personal data and Meta-derived data associated with your account within 30 days and confirm by email.
  3. Alternatively, log in to Advora → Settings → Account → Delete account to delete everything immediately.

A unique confirmation code is provided after a deletion request. You may also visit Facebook's instructions to remove Advora from your Business Integrations.

8. Children's privacy

The Service is not directed to anyone under 13 (or under 16 in the EU). We do not knowingly collect personal data from children.

9. International transfers

Your data may be processed in countries other than your own. Where required, we use appropriate safeguards such as Standard Contractual Clauses.

10. Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Material changes will be notified via email or in-app notice at least 14 days before they take effect.
